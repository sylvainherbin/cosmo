Fractal Cosmology Explained

In a Nutshell

This cosmological model uses a fractal structure based on the Fibonacci sequence to describe the universe. Unlike the standard model, it combines gravity, quantum mechanics, and the role of observers in a unified way. Starting from a point with no dimensions, the model grows into multiple universes through a special process, solving mysteries about the universe's expansion and structure.

Why a New Model?

In cosmology, scientists face puzzles like disagreements about the Hubble constant or unusual patterns in the cosmic microwave background (CMB). The standard model, ΛCDM, struggles to explain these issues. Our fractal model offers a solution by combining gravity, quantum mechanics, and the Fibonacci sequence.

The Starting Point

The universe begins as a single point with no dimensions. The genesis operator (\(\mathcal{O}\)) starts a chain reaction, creating time and space:

\[ \mathcal{O}(0) = 1, \quad \mathcal{O}(1) = -1 \] \[ F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2} \]

Here, \(F_n\) is the Fibonacci sequence, where each number is the sum of the two before it. The operator flips between positive and negative values to start the universe's growth.

Fibonacci & Golden Ratio

The Fibonacci sequence is a series of numbers:

\[ F_0 = 0, \quad F_1 = 1, \quad F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2} \]

As the sequence grows, the ratio of consecutive numbers approaches the golden ratio:

\[ \phi = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{F_n}{F_{n-1}} \approx 1.618 \]

This number, \(\phi\), shapes the universe's fractal structure—a pattern that repeats at different scales, like a snowflake.

Multiple Universes

The model suggests many universes, each following the Fibonacci pattern:

\[ \mathcal{U}_n = f_n(S_p, F_n) \] \[ \dim_{\mathscr{F}}(\mathcal{U}_n) \approx \phi \]

Here, \(\mathcal{U}_n\) represents a universe, and its "dimension" is close to the golden ratio, making it fractal. These universes are connected, like layers in a cosmic onion.

The Role of Observers

The model includes a mathematical rule that combines gravity and quantum mechanics:

\[ \mathcal{L}_n = \sqrt{-g} \left( \frac{R}{16\pi G} + \phi \langle \psi_n | \mathcal{O} | \psi_n \rangle \right) \]

This equation describes how the universe's shape (gravity, \(R\)) and tiny particles (quantum mechanics, \(\psi_n\)) work together, guided by the genesis operator and the golden ratio. Observers help define the universe's structure.

Key Predictions

This model makes specific predictions that scientists can test:

  • The cosmic microwave background (CMB) should show patterns matching the golden ratio (\(\alpha \approx 1.618 \pm 0.1\))
  • Galaxies should cluster following a related number (\(\gamma \approx 1.382 \pm 0.1\))
  • The golden ratio should be extremely stable (\(\delta \phi \sim 10^{-5}\))

These predictions will be tested with telescopes and experiments like CMB-S4 and Euclid in 2026-2027.
