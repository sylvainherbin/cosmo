Dynamic Fractal Cosmology

A Fibonacci Phase Transition Model for the Universe

Research Abstract

We present a complete fractal cosmological framework where the golden ratio $\phi$ evolves dynamically from primordial ($\phi_0=1.5$) to modern ($\phi_\infty=1.618$) epochs. This phase transition, characterized by rate parameter $\Gamma=0.23\pm0.01$, resolves the Hubble tension ($H_0=73.04\pm0.38$ km/s/Mpc) and explains CMB anomalies through scale-dependent fractal dimensions. The model predicts:

(1) BAO deviations $\Delta r_d/r_d \approx 0.15(1-e^{-z/2})$

(2) CMB power deficit $\mathcal{S}=0.93\pm0.02$ at $\ell<30$ ($\chi^2/\text{dof}=1.72$ vs $5.40$ for static fractal model with $\phi=1.5$ constant using Planck 2018 TT+lowE)

(3) Redshift-dependent growth $f(z)=\Omega_m(z)^{\phi(z)/2}$

DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.15973541

Key Features & Insights

  • Dynamic Golden Ratio (phi) Evolution: Introduces a phase transition for phi from a primordial value of 1.5 to its modern value of 1.618, driven by cosmic expansion.
  • Hubble Tension Resolution: The model naturally resolves the H0 tension with a prediction of H0=73.04±0.38 km/s/Mpc, aligning local and CMB measurements.
  • CMB Anomaly Explanation: Explains the observed CMB power deficit at large angular scales (l<30) through scale-dependent fractal dimensions.
  • Predictive Power for BAO: Foresees specific deviations in the Baryon Acoustic Oscillation (BAO) scale.
  • Improved Data Fit: Achieves a significantly better fit to Planck 2018 TT+lowE data (chi^2/dof=1.72) compared to static fractal models.
