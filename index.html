Fractal Cosmological Model

A novel approach to quantum gravity and cosmology through fractal geometry and the golden ratio

Abstract Overview

This research introduces a novel fractal cosmological model based on non-commutative geometry at Planck scales. The genesis operator \(\mathcal{O}\) generates Fibonacci sequences that structure a hierarchy of multiverses \(\mathcal{U}_n\) through temporal singularities converging to the golden ratio \(\phi \approx 1.618\).

The framework resolves fundamental cosmological problems, including the horizon problem, through inherent scale invariance and provides testable predictions for cosmic microwave background (CMB) observations. Key mathematical relationships include:

\[ \mathcal{L}_n = \sqrt{-g} \left( \frac{R}{16\pi G} + \phi \langle \psi_n | \mathcal{O} | \psi_n \rangle \right) \]

\[ \dim_{\mathscr{F}}(\mathcal{U}_n) \approx \phi \]

This model offers a unified description of quantum mechanics and general relativity through the lens of fractal geometry.

Key Features

  • Non-commutative geometry at Planck scales providing a quantum description of spacetime
  • Fractal dimension \(D = \frac{\ln\phi}{\ln 2}\) governing cosmic structure formation
  • Resolution of horizon problem through inherent scale invariance in the fractal geometry
  • Observer-dependent quantum gravity where measurement defines spacetime geometry
  • Golden ratio stability \(\delta \phi \sim 10^{-5}\) as a fundamental cosmological constant

Mathematical Foundation

The model is built on a mathematical framework combining Fibonacci sequences with quantum operators:

\[ \mathcal{O}(0) = 1, \quad \mathcal{O}(1) = -1 \]

\[ F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2} \]

Quantum Gravity

Integration of quantum mechanics with general relativity through a fractal approach:

\[ \phi \langle \psi_n | \mathcal{O} | \psi_n \rangle \]

where \(\psi_n\) represents quantum states at different fractal scales.

Multiverse Structure

Hierarchical organization of multiverses governed by fractal geometry:

\[ \mathcal{U}_n = f_n(S_p, F_n) \]

Each universe \(\mathcal{U}_n\) possesses a fractal dimension approximating \(\phi\).
