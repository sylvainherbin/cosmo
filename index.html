A New Paradigm in Cosmology

For decades, the standard cosmological model, $\Lambda$CDM, has provided an excellent framework for understanding the universe. However, recent precise measurements have revealed significant discrepancies, known as "cosmic tensions," challenging our fundamental understanding of the cosmos.

Dynamic Fractal Cosmology offers a revolutionary perspective. By introducing a dynamic fractal dimension that evolves with the universe, this model provides a novel framework to address these tensions and opens up new avenues for exploring the universe's origin and evolution. We propose that the universe's structure is fundamentally fractal, governed by the golden ratio, which dynamically changes over cosmic time.

At the heart of Dynamic Fractal Cosmology is the evolution of the universe's **fractal dimension**, $\phi$, which changes with redshift ($z$) according to:

\[ \phi(z) = \phi_{\infty} - (\phi_{\infty} - \phi_0)e^{-\Gamma z} \]

This equation describes a **cosmic phase transition**, where the universe's fundamental fractal nature shifts from a primordial state ($\phi_0$) to its current form ($\phi_{\infty}$) over cosmic time. This dynamic behavior offers a fresh perspective on the universe's expansion and structure formation, potentially resolving long-standing cosmic mysteries.

Addressing Cosmic Tensions

Our model specifically tackles two of the most pressing issues in modern cosmology:

The Hubble Tension

There's a significant disagreement in the measured value of the Hubble constant ($H_0$), which describes the universe's expansion rate. Early-universe measurements (from the Cosmic Microwave Background) conflict with local-universe measurements (from supernovae). Our dynamic fractal approach offers a unique way to reconcile these values by proposing a scale-dependent expansion rate.

CMB Anomalies

Subtle anomalies in the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) radiation, particularly at large angular scales (low-$\ell$ multipoles), remain unexplained by the standard model. Our fractal geometry naturally introduces corrections that align with these observed patterns, providing a more complete picture of the early universe.

Explore the Research

Our research delves deep into the mathematical framework and observational tests of Dynamic Fractal Cosmology. We utilize extensive datasets from Baryon Acoustic Oscillations (BAO) and Type Ia Supernovae (SNIa) to constrain our model's parameters, demonstrating an exceptional fit to current cosmological data.
